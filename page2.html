HOW TO RECOVER QUICKLY AFTER INJURY

Giving it Time to Heal


Once you have properly diagnosed your injury, next you must figure out what it needs and give it the time it needs to heal. As I mentioned before all injuries are different and require different care. With this in mind, they still all require a combination of rest and exercise, knowing how much of each is key.

Finding the perfect combination of these two aspects, depending on the severity of your injury, is the base of the healing process. Focusing too much on resting will lead to a slower and weak recovery, often resulting in re-injuring the area. On the other hand, moving too much too soon can worsen the injury and will significantly affect your recovery. Because of this, it is hard to give a generic healing plan but a few things that physiotherapists suggest are applying the technique RICE and executing moderate movement.

RICE is a four step process that involves rest, ice, compression and elevation. These four points are needed by common injuries such as sprains.

After protecting the injured area for the first 3 to 5 days, physical therapists suggest moving your injury in order to improve blood circulation and promote healing.
